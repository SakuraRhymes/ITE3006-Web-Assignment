Sun

The Sun—the heart of our solar system—is a yellow dwarf star, a hot ball of glowing gases.The connection and interactions between the Sun and Earth drive the seasons, ocean currents, weather, climate, radiation belts and aurorae.

Did You Know?


The Sun’s visible surface sometimes has dark sunspots, which are areas of intense magnetic activity that can lead to solar explosions.If the Sun were as tall as a typical front door, Earth would be about the size of a nickel.
